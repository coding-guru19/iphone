Keep Learning on Track

Education
  • Create a 30–60 minute daily study block; phone in another room.
  • Use free resources (past papers, YouTube explainers, library Wi-Fi).
  • Ask teachers for a term plan and check progress weekly.
  • Find a study buddy or weekend community class.
Find resources

See it. Name it. Stop it.

Bullying
  • Document dates/screenshots. Don’t respond with violence.
  • Report to a teacher/grade head. Escalate to principal if needed.
  • Block/report cyberbullying and keep evidence.
  • Ask for a safety plan: seating changes, supervision, check-ins.
Report channels

Gender-Based Violence

GBVF
  • If in danger: get to a safe place. Call emergency services.
  • Tell someone you trust. Save evidence (photos, messages).
  • Seek medical care within 72h for injuries or PEP.
  • Apply for a protection order; ask a counsellor for guidance.
Shelters & legal aid

Support, not shame

Teen Pregnancy
  • Book a clinic visit early for antenatal checks.
  • Ask the school for continued-learning arrangements.
  • Get info on rights, grants, and parenting support.
  • If not pregnant: learn contraception options and consent.
Clinics & info

Break the loop

Substance Abuse
  • Tell one trusted person and remove immediate triggers.
  • Swap the habit window with exercise or chores.
  • Use counselling/support groups; ask about rehab referrals.
  • Track streaks; reward progress weekly.
Hotlines & programmes

Know your status. Protect your future.

HIV/AIDS
  • Test regularly if sexually active; use protection correctly.
  • Ask clinics about PrEP (before exposure) or PEP (within 72h after risk).
  • ART with undetectable viral load = much lower transmission risk.
  • Combine prevention: condoms + testing + honest conversations.
